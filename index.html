Gender Is Like A Sweater

This a talk given to Worcester Polytechnic Institute's Diversity in Games Club.

This presentation explores gender identity and diversity in the context of modern media. It will cover topics such as the complexity of "passing," what it means to medically transition, and problematic representation. This talk will give insight into how to respectfully portray trans characters in games and discuss trans issues in everyday life.

About

I openly identify as a transgender man. I feel that my unique social perspective is an important element of the creativity that I bring to any project. My voice is something fresh in our community of game developers a nd I hope to use it to break the cycle of prejudice and misrepresentation in the games that I help craft. Beyond this, I'm a junior at Worcester Polytechnic Institute pursuing a degree in Interactive Media and Game Design. I have a soft spot for outrageously hot coffee and start each day by actually making my bed.

Why do I make games?

Well, I make games because I have to. Not because I’m being mandated by some academic assignment or external force, but because they are inside of me. I can’t think of anything else on this earth that I love more than developing, creating, or writing an artifact of entertainment. I also think that games provide a unique way to interact with an audience. You have the opportunity to do things like empower them, frustrate them, or enlighten them. You afford them the ability to take an active role in some narrative that has the potential to shift their perspective.

What kind of games do I want to make?

I want to make games that change the way people think. I want to make games that introduce potentially ignorant players to social and economic minorities that routinely face discrimination. I want to make games that disarm hatred and spread acceptance.
